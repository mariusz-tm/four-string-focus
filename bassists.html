1970's

Born September 6, 1943, Waters is renowned for his co-founding of the rock band Pink Floyd in 1965. He released 8 albums while in Pink Floyd before leaving in 1985. Notably in 1990, Waters staged one of the largest rock concerts in history, with an attendance of 450,000. Recently, Waters continues his music career solo.

Waters played a pivotal role as the primary lyricist and conceptual force behind many of the band's most acclaimed works, including albums that explored themes of isolation, war, and societal critique. His creative vision helped shape the band’s identity, particularly during the 1970s, as their music grew more ambitious and philosophically driven. Despite internal tensions within the group, Waters' influence remained central to their evolving sound and stage productions.

Following his departure from the band, Waters pursued a solo career that allowed him to further develop his distinctive narrative style and political commentary. His live performances became known for their elaborate visuals and strong thematic messages, often reflecting his personal views on global issues. Over the years, he has maintained a dedicated fan base and continues to be recognized as a significant figure in the history of rock music.

Roger Waters

1980's

Born November 13, 1957, Sakurai is well famed for his founding of the jazz fusion band Casiopea in 1976, alongside 野呂 一生 (Noro Issei). He released 19 albums while in Casiopea before leaving in 1989. Nowadays, Sakurai is a member of a group called Katsushika Trio alongside 2 other ex-Casiopea members.

Sakurai served as a key contributor to the band's rhythmic foundation and musical direction during its formative and most prolific years. As a bassist, his technical precision and melodic sensibility became defining elements of the group's signature jazz fusion sound. Throughout his tenure, the band gained both domestic and international recognition, with their tightly arranged compositions and virtuosic performances earning widespread acclaim.

After departing from the group, Sakurai continued to explore his musical interests through various collaborations and projects, maintaining a presence within the jazz and fusion scenes. In recent years, his involvement with Katsushika Trio has marked a return to working closely with former bandmates, blending nostalgia with continued musical innovation. His enduring influence reflects a career built on consistency, craftsmanship, and a deep commitment to his genre.

桜井 哲夫 (Sakurai Tetsuo)

1990's - 1990's

Born October 16, 1962, Michael Peter Balzary is best known as the bassist and co-founder of the rock band Red Hot Chili Peppers, for which he writes, performs, and occasionally produces music. He has released 13 albums with the Red Hot Chili Peppers since their formation in 1983. Alongside his work with the band, Balzary has collaborated and recorded with artists such as Johnny Cash, The Mars Volta, Thom Yorke, and Alanis Morissette.

Balzary, widely recognized by his stage name Flea, has been instrumental in shaping the band's distinctive sound through his energetic bass playing and genre-blending approach. His style, which combines funk-driven grooves with elements of punk and rock, has become a defining characteristic of the band's music. Over the decades, his dynamic stage presence and musical versatility have contributed significantly to the group's enduring popularity and evolving artistic direction.

Beyond his work with the band, Balzary has established himself as a respected collaborator across a wide range of musical styles. His contributions to various projects highlight his adaptability and willingness to experiment with different sounds and creative approaches. In addition to performing, he has occasionally taken on production roles, further showcasing his broad skill set and continued influence within the music industry.

Michael Peter Balzary

1990's

Born November 12, 1964, Dave Ellefson is best known as the bassist and co-founder of the thrash metal band Megadeth, for which he performs and co-writes music. He released 16 albums with Megadeth across his tenures with the band between 1983 and 2021. Alongside his work with Megadeth, Ellefson has collaborated with artists such as Metal Allegiance, Temple of Brutality, F5, and Killing Machine.

Ellefson has been a foundational figure in shaping the aggressive and technically precise sound of the band through his fast, intricate bass playing and tight rhythmic coordination. As both a performer and co-writer, he contributed to the development of the band’s signature thrash metal style, characterized by complex song structures and high-energy performances. His role remained integral across multiple eras of the band, helping to maintain consistency while adapting to changes in lineup and musical direction.

Outside of his work with the band, Ellefson has remained active in the broader metal community through a range of collaborative projects and musical ventures. His involvement with various groups has allowed him to explore different interpretations of heavy music while continuing to refine his craft. Through these efforts, he has sustained a long-standing presence in the genre, earning recognition for both his musicianship and his contributions to the evolution of thrash metal.

Dave Ellefson

2000's

Born April 16 1969, Pilichowski is a famed Polish bassist, being recognised as the best Polish bass player through numerous nominations and awards nationally. Recording his first solo album in 1994, Pilichowski has since released 20 solo albums, and over 200 total albums through his collaboration with other artists.

Pilichowski has established himself as one of the most prominent figures in the Polish music scene through his technical mastery and distinctive approach to the bass guitar. His playing style blends elements of funk, jazz, and rock, resulting in a sound that is both versatile and immediately recognizable. Over the years, his dedication to his craft has earned him widespread acclaim, with numerous awards and honors highlighting his influence and reputation within his home country.

Beyond his solo career, Pilichowski has been an exceptionally prolific collaborator, contributing to a vast number of recordings across a wide range of genres. His ability to adapt to different musical contexts while maintaining his unique voice has made him a highly respected figure among fellow musicians. Through both his solo work and extensive collaborations, he continues to shape and inspire the bass community with his consistency, creativity, and passion for music.

Wojtek Pilichowski

2000's - 2010's

Born January 20, 1986, Parker is best known for his musical project Tame Impala, for which he writes, performs, records, and produces the music. He released 5 albums under Tame Impala. Alongside his work for Tame Impala, Parker has collaborated as a producer with artists such as Lady Gaga, Gorillaz, Rihanna, and Travis Scott.

He first gained widespread recognition with the release of Innerspeaker in 2010, followed by Lonerism in 2012, both of which were praised for their psychedelic sound and introspective songwriting. His third album, Currents (2015), marked a shift toward a more synth-driven, pop-oriented style and brought him mainstream success. This evolution continued with The Slow Rush in 2020, further solidifying his reputation as a versatile and innovative artist.

In addition to his studio work, Parker is known for his meticulous approach to sound design and his ability to blend analog and digital recording techniques. His influence extends beyond his own releases, as his production style has shaped the sound of contemporary psychedelic and alternative music. Through both Tame Impala and his collaborations, he has established himself as one of the most distinctive and sought-after musicians and producers of his generation.

Kevin Parker

2010's

Born June 18, 1979, Rafael Navejas is best known as the bassist of the rock band Enjambre, for which he performs and contributes to songwriting. He has released 8 albums with Enjambre since joining the group. Alongside his work with the band, Navejas has collaborated with various artists within the Latin alternative and rock music scenes.

Navejas has played a significant role in shaping the band's sound through his melodic bass lines and steady rhythmic foundation. His playing complements the group's distinctive blend of alternative rock and retro-inspired influences, adding depth and cohesion to their music. As both a performer and contributor to songwriting, he has helped define the band's evolving style while maintaining a strong connection to their core musical identity.

In addition to his work with the band, Navejas has remained active within the broader Latin alternative and rock scenes through various collaborations. These projects have allowed him to explore different musical directions while refining his approach as a bassist. His continued involvement across multiple creative efforts highlights his versatility and commitment to his craft, solidifying his place within contemporary Latin rock music.

Rafael Navejas
